Not every business is ready to automate. Here's how to figure out if yours is — and where to start if it is.

## You keep doing the same thing over and over

The clearest sign you're ready is repetition. If you or your team perform the same sequence of steps more than a few times a week — sending follow-up emails, updating spreadsheets, generating reports — that's a process waiting to be automated. The key word is "sequence." A single task isn't worth automating. A chain of tasks absolutely is.

## You've already documented (some of) your processes

Automation doesn't create processes. It accelerates existing ones. If your team already follows a rough playbook for things like onboarding, invoicing, or project handoffs, you have something to work with. If everything lives in people's heads, step one is getting it on paper — not building a Zapier flow.

## You're losing time, not just money

Most business owners think about automation in terms of cost savings. But the real unlock is time. If you're spending three hours a week on reporting that could take five minutes, that's three hours you're not spending on strategy, sales, or simply thinking. The businesses that benefit most from automation aren't the biggest — they're the ones where the founder's time is the bottleneck.

## You don't need to automate everything at once

Start with one workflow. The one that frustrates you the most, or the one that breaks the most often. Get that running smoothly, then look for the next one. Automation compounds — each system you build frees up capacity to build the next.

## The honest answer

If you're Googling "should I automate my business," you're probably ready. The fact that you're thinking about systems means you've outgrown doing everything manually. The question isn't if — it's where to start.
