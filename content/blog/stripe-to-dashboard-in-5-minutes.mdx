A walkthrough of how I connected Stripe to a live dashboard without writing code — and why it changed how I look at revenue.

## The problem

I was logging into Stripe multiple times a day to check on payments, failed charges, and MRR. It's not that Stripe's interface is bad — it's great. But I didn't need all of it. I needed three numbers, and I needed them visible without clicking through menus.

## The setup

I used a simple pipeline: Stripe webhook sends payment events to a lightweight backend, which pushes the data into a Google Sheet. From there, a dashboard tool pulls the numbers and displays them. Total setup time was about five minutes once I knew what I wanted to see. No code. No complex API work. Just connecting dots.

## What the dashboard shows

Three things. Monthly recurring revenue, number of active subscriptions, and failed payment rate. That's it. I don't need charts or trend lines for daily decisions — I need a pulse check. If MRR drops or failed payments spike, I see it immediately without hunting for it.

## Why this matters beyond convenience

Before the dashboard, I was reactive. I'd discover a billing issue when a client emailed. Now I catch it same-day, often before the client even notices. That single change improved retention more than any marketing effort I ran that quarter.

## The bigger lesson

Most reporting automation isn't about building fancy dashboards. It's about surfacing the right numbers in a place you'll actually look. Start with what you check most often, and make it effortless to see.
