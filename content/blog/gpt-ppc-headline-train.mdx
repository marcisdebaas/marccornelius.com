I used GPT to generate 200 PPC headlines in under ten minutes. Here's the process, what worked, and what still needed a human touch.

## The headline bottleneck

Writing PPC headlines is tedious. You need variations, you need to hit character limits, and you need to test angles you wouldn't normally think of. For a single responsive search ad you want at least 10-15 headlines. Scale that across campaigns and ad groups, and you're looking at hundreds. That's a lot of creative energy spent on 30-character strings.

## The process

I gave GPT a structured prompt: the product, the target audience, the key benefits, and the character limit. Then I asked for batches of 20 headlines at a time, each with a different angle — urgency, social proof, price, features, pain points. In ten minutes I had a spreadsheet with over 200 options. Most of them were usable. About 30% were genuinely good.

## What worked

GPT is excellent at generating volume and variety. It came up with angles I wouldn't have considered — different ways to frame the same benefit, unexpected word choices, creative use of limited character space. The "headline train" approach — generating in rapid batches without stopping to edit — produced far more usable material than my usual method of staring at a blank field.

## What didn't

Some headlines were generic. "Transform Your Business Today" type of stuff that sounds like every other ad. GPT also struggled with brand voice — the outputs were competent but lacked the specific tone we use in our campaigns. And it occasionally broke character limits despite being told the constraint.

## The human layer

The best workflow isn't fully automated. Generate the bulk with GPT, then curate with human judgment. I spent about 20 minutes reviewing the 200 headlines, cutting the weak ones, tweaking the good ones, and flagging the best for testing. Total time: 30 minutes for what used to take half a day.
