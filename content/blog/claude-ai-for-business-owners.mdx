Claude isn't just another chatbot. Here's what it can actually do for your business — and where it fits in your workflow.

## What Claude is

Claude is an AI assistant built by Anthropic. If you've used ChatGPT, the concept is familiar — you type a question or instruction, and it responds. But Claude has some characteristics that make it particularly useful for business applications: it handles long documents well, it follows complex instructions reliably, and it's less likely to make things up when it doesn't know something.

## Where it fits in a business workflow

The most practical use cases aren't the flashy ones. It's not about generating images or writing novels. It's about the daily work that eats your time. Summarizing a 40-page contract and highlighting the key terms. Drafting email responses in your tone of voice. Analyzing a spreadsheet of customer feedback and categorizing the themes. Turning meeting notes into action items. These are the tasks where Claude saves you real hours.

## Claude with tools (MCP)

Where it gets really interesting is when Claude connects to your existing tools through MCP — Model Context Protocol. Instead of copying data into a chat window, Claude can query your Google Ads account, pull data from your CRM, or check your project management tool directly. This turns it from a helpful chatbot into an actual business tool that works with your data in real time.

## What it doesn't do

Claude won't replace your team. It won't make strategic decisions for you. And it won't magically understand your business without context. Like any tool, it's as useful as the instructions you give it. The business owners who get the most value from AI are the ones who take time to set it up properly — clear prompts, connected tools, defined workflows.

## Getting started

You don't need a technical background to start using Claude. Begin with one task you do repeatedly — drafting proposals, summarizing reports, analyzing data — and try it. See how the output compares to doing it manually. Most people find that even with some editing, the time savings are significant. From there, you can expand to more complex workflows as you learn what works.
