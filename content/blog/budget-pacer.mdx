Overspending on ads because you forgot to check the budget? A simple budget pacer script keeps your campaigns on track without daily babysitting.

## The problem with daily budgets

Google Ads lets you set daily budgets, but it also reserves the right to overspend by up to 2x on any given day. Over a month it's supposed to even out, but in practice, spend can spike unpredictably — especially on high-volume campaigns. If you're managing budgets across multiple accounts, keeping track of monthly pacing manually is a headache.

## What a budget pacer does

A budget pacer checks your actual spend against where you should be at this point in the month. If it's the 15th and you've already spent 70% of your monthly budget, something needs to adjust. The script calculates the ideal daily spend for the remaining days and either alerts you or adjusts budgets automatically to stay on track.

## How I built mine

The logic is straightforward. Pull month-to-date spend from the Google Ads API. Compare it to the monthly target. Calculate the remaining daily budget needed. If the variance is above a threshold, send a Slack notification with the recommended adjustment. The whole thing runs as a scheduled script every morning before campaigns ramp up for the day.

## Why this beats manual checking

Humans forget. Especially on Fridays, holidays, and during busy weeks. A budget pacer doesn't forget. It runs every single day and catches pacing issues before they become overspend problems. Over a year, this has saved my clients thousands of euros in wasted spend simply by keeping things steady.

## Start simple

You don't need a fancy tool for this. A Google Sheets script connected to your ads data can work as a first version. The important thing is that something checks your pacing every day — whether that's a script, a tool, or a calendar reminder. Automation just makes it reliable.
