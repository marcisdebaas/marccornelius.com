Everyone's uploading spreadsheets to ChatGPT for analysis. But should you? Here's what you need to know about privacy, data retention, and practical alternatives.

## The appeal is obvious

You have a CSV with 10,000 rows of campaign data. Instead of wrestling with pivot tables, you upload it to ChatGPT and ask, "Which campaigns had the best ROAS last month?" Within seconds you get a clear answer. It's fast, it's easy, and it feels like magic. No wonder people are doing it.

## The privacy question

When you upload data to ChatGPT, it's processed on OpenAI's servers. If you're using the free tier or the Plus plan without opting out, your data may be used to train future models. That means your client's campaign performance, spend data, and potentially identifying information could end up in a training dataset. For business data, that's a real concern.

## What OpenAI says

With a Team or Enterprise plan, OpenAI states that your data is not used for training. API usage also doesn't train models by default. So the answer to "is it safe?" depends heavily on which plan you're on and how you're accessing the tool. Most small business owners uploading spreadsheets are on Plus — which has weaker guarantees.

## Practical alternatives

If you want AI-powered analysis without sending data to external servers, there are options. Running a local model is one. Using the API with explicit data handling agreements is another. Or you can anonymize your data before uploading — strip client names, replace identifiers with codes, and remove anything that isn't strictly needed for the analysis.

## The bottom line

Using AI to analyze campaign data is smart. Doing it without thinking about where that data goes is not. Check your plan, read the terms, and when in doubt, anonymize first. Your clients trust you with their data — make sure that trust is warranted.
