What happens when you connect Google Ads to Claude using MCP? You get a conversational interface to your ad data — and it's surprisingly useful.

## What is MCP?

Model Context Protocol is a way to give AI models like Claude access to external tools and data sources. Instead of copying data into a chat window, the model can query your systems directly. In this case, I connected Claude to the Google Ads API via an MCP server, which means I can ask questions about my campaigns and get real answers from real data.

## What this looks like in practice

Instead of opening the Google Ads interface, navigating to the right campaign, setting the date range, and reading through columns of numbers, I type something like "How did my search campaigns perform last week compared to the week before?" Claude queries the API, pulls the data, and gives me a summary. Impressions, clicks, cost, conversions — with context about what changed and why it might matter.

## Where it gets interesting

The real value isn't just pulling numbers. It's asking follow-up questions. "Which ad groups had the highest CPA?" "Are there any keywords spending more than 50 euros with zero conversions?" "Show me the trend for my brand campaigns over the last 30 days." Each of these would take several clicks in the Google Ads UI. Here, it's a sentence.

## The limitations

This isn't a replacement for the full Google Ads interface. You're not going to build campaigns or adjust bids through a chat window — at least not yet. It's a reporting and analysis layer. Think of it as a faster way to get answers from your data without context-switching out of your workflow.

## Why this matters for business owners

Most business owners don't check their ad performance as often as they should because the tools are complex and time-consuming to navigate. A conversational interface lowers that barrier dramatically. You're more likely to stay on top of your numbers when checking them takes ten seconds instead of ten minutes.
